In recent years a lot more people have been identifying as neurodivergent. The main impact of this is that more people are making demands for recognition and rights that allow us to push back against the productivist norms of capitalist states.  
近年来，越来越多的人开始认同自己为神经殊异者。其主要影响在于，更多人开始要求获得认可与权利，从而能够抵制资本主义国家中生产主义规范的压迫。

As such a new backlash against neurodiversity has also emerged, with representatives of ruling class ideology - often reactionary psychologists, psychiatrists, journalists, and politicians - seeking to delegitimise the identifications of neurodivergent people. The point of this is to undermine neurodivergent power as well as access to rights and recognition.  
因此，针对神经多样性的新一轮抵制也随之出现。统治阶级意识形态的代表——通常是反动的心理学家、精神病学家、记者和政治家——试图否定神经殊异者的自我认同。其目的在于削弱神经殊异者的力量，并剥夺他们获得权利与认可的机会。

One of the most common and superficially viable points made by proponents of ruling class ideology here is that as more people identify as neurodivergent, the term has less meaning. Hence these people say things like "if everyone is neurodivergent, then nobody is!", presenting this as if it was an obvious conceptual truism that necessarily limits how many neurodivergent people there can be.  
统治阶级意识形态支持者在此提出的一个最常见且表面上看似合理的论点是：随着越来越多的人认同为神经殊异者，这一术语的意义正在减弱。因此，这些人会说诸如"如果每个人都是神经殊异者，那么就没有人是神经殊异者！"之类的话，仿佛这是一个显而易见的概念性真理，必然限制了神经殊异者可能存在的数量。

The main problem with this line of reasoning is that there is no principled justification for thinking that “neurodivergent” only has meaning if it refers to a minority of the population. In fact, there is no conceptual limit on how many people can be neurodivergent as such. It is perfectly conceptually feasible that everyone could be neurodivergent.  
这种推理的主要问题在于，认为"神经殊异"只有指代少数人群才有意义，这种观点缺乏原则性的依据。事实上，从概念层面而言，成为神经殊异者的人数并不存在上限。从概念上讲，完全有可能所有人都属于神经殊异群体。

We do need a caveat here: this is only feasible if people are neurodivergent in different ways. It would be impossible for everyone to be neurodivergent in the same way, since any specific form of neurodivergence is determined in relation to social, statistical, and functional norms. So, conceptually, it is not the case that everyone in the world could have, say, the exact same form of intellectual disability, since if that were the case then the norm, and who counts as disabled in this way, would change too.  
这里确实需要说明一点：只有当人们以不同方式呈现神经殊异性时，这种可能性才成立。所有人以完全相同的方式呈现神经殊异性是不可能的，因为任何特定形式的神经殊异性都是相对于社会、统计和功能规范来界定的。因此从概念上讲，世界上不可能所有人都患有完全相同的智力障碍——倘若真是如此，那么规范标准以及被认定为这类障碍者的群体也会随之改变。

But it is perfectly conceptually possible for everyone to be neurodivergent if this is spread out through different forms of neurodivergence (dyslexia, autism, etc.). So long as each of these forms remains outside neuronormativity, there is no reason in principle to discount the logical possibility of a situation where everyone alive was neurodivergent, albeit in many different ways.  
但从概念上讲，如果神经殊异性以不同形式（如阅读障碍、孤独谱系等）分布，那么所有人都有可能成为神经殊异者。只要这些形式仍处于神经常态之外，原则上就没有理由否定这样一种逻辑可能性：即所有在世者都是神经殊异者，尽管表现形式各不相同。

If this sounds strange consider an analogy: pretty much all people (basically everyone except new born babies) have been physically ill. We haven't had the same illnesses as each other, perhaps, but everyone has been ill in one way or another, and this doesn't mean that "illness" thereby loses it's meaning. It is perfectly meaningful even though all people become ill sooner or later. It's the same with “neurodivergent” or “neurodivergence”.  
如果这听起来有些奇怪，不妨考虑一个类比：几乎所有人（基本上除了新生儿之外）都曾经历过身体不适。我们可能没有得过完全相同的疾病，但每个人都以某种方式生过病，而这并不意味着“疾病”因此就失去了意义。即使所有人迟早都会生病，“疾病”这个词依然具有明确的意义。同样，“神经殊异”或“神经殊异性”也是如此。

Note here that I'm just talking about what's feasible conceptually. I'm not claiming that everyone actually is in fact neurodivergent. My point is that there is no natural or conceptual limit on how many people can be considered neurodivergent. Attempts to dismiss increased recognition by claiming the word is becoming meaningless are merely ideological and have no principled basis.  
请注意，这里我只是在讨论概念上的可行性，并非声称每个人实际上都是神经殊异者。我的观点是，对于被视为神经殊异者的人数，并不存在自然或概念上的限制。那些试图通过声称这个词正在变得毫无意义来否定日益增长的认知的做法，仅仅是意识形态上的，并无原则性依据。
