The neurodiversity movement is often described as a “civil rights” movement (including by me). This isn’t wrong. But I’ve come to see that it can be misleading.  
神经多样性运动常被描述为一场“民权”运动（包括我自己也曾如此表述）。这并非错误。但我逐渐意识到，这种说法可能具有误导性。

What the description captures is the movement’s orientation toward rights: its insistence that neurodivergent people should be legally entitled to recognition, accommodation, and support. What it risks obscuring is how those rights have been pursued. Unlike earlier civil rights movements, neurodiversity activism has rarely been about forcing the state to legislate new protections. It has instead taken place on a terrain where those protections, at least formally, already exist. The neurodiversity movement, in other words, is downstream of the disability rights gains of the late twentieth and early twenty-first centuries. It has not sought to found a new juridical order; rather, it has worked within an inherited one. In this context, its innovation has been less legal than cultural.  
上述描述捕捉到了该运动对权利的取向：即坚持神经殊异人群应在法律上享有被认可、获得合理便利和支持的权利。但它可能掩盖的是这些权利是如何被争取的。与早期的民权运动不同，神经多样性行动主义很少致力于迫使国家立法制定新的保护措施。相反，它发生在一个至少形式上已存在这些保护的领域。换言之，神经多样性运动是二十世纪末至二十一世纪初残障权利成果的后续产物。它并非试图建立新的司法秩序，而是在继承的秩序框架内运作。在此背景下，其创新更多体现在文化层面而非法律层面。

More specifically, its central operation has been to expand the field of those who count and also how we count. Conceptually, psychiatric diagnoses once framed purely as disorders or illnesses have been re-described as disabilities. This is without necessarily shedding their medical character, so long as the status of disability is made central so that rights-based demands could be made. At the same time, the boundaries of these diagnoses have themselves been loosened, especially as more people come to self-identify as neurodivergent. Categories such as ADHD stretch and admit more people who may previously have sat just outside their borders. On a mass level, individuals who had previously understood their suffering as private failure have thus come to recognise it as structured, shared, and more politicisable.  
更具体地说，其核心运作在于扩大"谁被计入"以及"如何被计入"的范畴。在概念层面，原本纯粹被界定为障碍或疾病的精神科诊断，已被重新描述为残障。这未必意味着完全剥离其医学属性，只要残障状态被置于核心位置，就能提出基于权利的主张。与此同时，这些诊断的边界本身也在松动——尤其当越来越多人开始自我认同为神经殊异者时。诸如 ADHD（注意缺陷多动障碍）这类诊断范畴不断扩展，接纳了更多原本可能处于其边界之外的人群。在群体层面，那些曾将自身痛苦理解为私人失败的个体，由此得以认识到这种痛苦具有结构性、共享性，且更易被政治化。

The most important result, however, is not semantic or about things like self-acceptance: it is material. Millions of struggling people who had been left to endure in silence, often for decades before dying deaths of despair, have instead found a vocabulary through which to make rights-based claims. For some, this means educational support; for others, workplace adjustments; for many, simply the difference between total immiseration and something slightly more bearable. While this is not emancipation, it is mitigation. And mitigation, under contemporary conditions, is important.  
然而，最重要的成果并非语义层面或自我接纳之类的问题，而是物质层面的。数百万长期在沉默中挣扎、往往在绝望中度过数十年直至死去的人们，终于找到了一种语言，得以提出基于权利的主张。对一些人而言，这意味着教育支持；对另一些人，则是工作场所的调整；对许多人来说，这甚至是从彻底贫困到稍可忍受之间的差别。虽然这并非解放，却是一种缓解。而在当代条件下，缓解本身至关重要。

To understand why this strategy emerged, and why it worked to the extent it has, we also must situate it within a longer political-economic arc. As I argued in *[Empire of Normalit](https://www.plutobooks.com/product/empire-of-normality/) y*, the rise of neurodiversity discourse coincides with the neoliberal restructuring of work and life that began in the 1980s. From that point, trade unions were weakened, worker protections were rolled back, and labour became more precarious and individualised. At the same time, work itself changed. The shift toward service and attention economies meant that what was sold was no longer labour power in the classical sense, but more primarily cognition, affect, and personality. Today’s worker must feel correctly, respond correctly, perform a continuous, monitored normality. Technologies of surveillance intensify this demand.  
要理解这一策略为何出现，以及为何能在一定程度上奏效，我们还必须将其置于更长的政治经济脉络中审视。正如我在《常态帝国》中所论证的，神经多样性话语的兴起与 20 世纪 80 年代开始的新自由主义对工作与生活的重构同步发生。从那时起，工会力量被削弱，工人保护措施被削减，劳动变得更加不稳定和个体化。与此同时，工作本身也发生了变化。向服务经济和注意力经济的转型意味着，被出售的不再是经典意义上的劳动力，而更多是认知、情感和人格。今天的劳动者必须正确感受、正确回应，持续表演一种被监控的常态。监控技术加剧了这一要求。

Under these conditions, neuronormativity rapidly tightened. The acceptable bandwidth of thought, feeling, and behaviour narrowed, and more and more people found themselves unable to conform. This was not because they have changed, but because the norms did. This is why, in *[Empire of Normality](https://www.plutobooks.com/product/empire-of-normality/)*, I described post-Fordism as a mass disabling event. It did not just bring a surge in anxiety and depression but also a very real rise in neurodivergent disablement.  
在这种条件下，神经规范性迅速收紧。思想、情感与行为的可接受范围不断收窄，越来越多的人发现自己无法适应。这并非因为他们改变了，而是因为规范本身发生了变化。正因如此，我在《常态帝国》中将后福特主义描述为一场大规模致残事件。它不仅带来了焦虑与抑郁的激增，更导致了神经殊异性残障状况的真实增长。

Within this landscape, the neurodiversity movement can be understood as a kind of compensatory mechanism. Where collective labour protections were eroded, individualised rights claims on the basis of protected identities proliferated. Where work and other aspects of life became more punishing, more people sought refuge in the category of neurodivergence as a way to make sense of these material conditions and to resist new forms of neuronormative domination.  
在此背景下，神经多样性运动可被理解为一种补偿机制。当集体劳动保护被侵蚀时，基于受保护身份的个体化权利诉求便大量涌现。当工作及其他生活领域变得愈发严苛时，更多人将神经殊异性范畴视为庇护所，以此理解这些物质条件并抵抗新型神经规范统治。

And, for a time, the strategy worked, at least as much as any identity politics-based demands did. Not perfectly or universally to be sure, but enough to matter. Millions of people who might once have experienced themselves as defective came to see themselves instead as entitled to basic levels of dignity, to support, to accommodation, to a different relation to the demands placed upon them. The system remained intact, but its sharpest edges were in many cases blunted.  
曾几何时，这一策略确实奏效了——至少与任何基于身份政治的主张相比毫不逊色。当然并非尽善尽美或放之四海皆准，但已足以产生实质影响。数百万曾自认有缺陷的人，转而开始相信自己有权获得基本尊严、支持、便利条件，以及与外界要求不同的相处方式。体系本身虽未动摇，但其最锋利的棱角在许多情况下已被磨平。

## Why These Strategies Are Becoming Less Effective为何这些策略正逐渐失效

As a neurodivergent person on the left rather than a liberal, I have often made (usually friendly) critiques of the analysis and strategies of the movement. While, as per above, I recognise the very real successes of these strategies, I have [argued](https://www.bostonreview.net/forum/the-future-of-neurodiversity/) that it nonetheless faces the limitations common to all forms of identity politics under neoliberalism. As such I’ve argued that locating capitalism as the underlying issue might help neurodivergent people gain a deeper understanding of our shared interests, and reorient our politics against capitalism rather than just some of its effects. That was the kind of argument I made most extensively in *Empire of Normality*, and I am yet to hear a convincing argument against it.  
作为一名左翼而非自由派的神经殊异者，我时常（通常以友善的方式）对该运动的分析与策略提出批评。尽管如上所述，我承认这些策略确实取得了切实的成功，但我认为它们仍面临新自由主义下所有身份政治形式共有的局限。因此，我主张将资本主义定位为根本问题，或许能帮助神经殊异者更深刻地理解我们的共同利益，并将我们的政治方向转向反对资本主义本身，而不仅仅是其某些后果。这正是我在《常态帝国》一书中详尽阐述的论点，至今尚未听到令人信服的反驳。

But in recent years, as I gestured towards in the book but didn’t cover in detail, something else has felt more pressing. This regards how the entire strategies of the neurodiversity movement presuppose a world in which rights claims can still be heard. More specifically, it presupposes a liberal, rules-based order in which the language of rights retains legitimacy: where institutions, however imperfect, can be appealed to, and where recognition can translate, however unevenly, into material concessions.  
但近年来，正如我在书中提及却未详述的那样，某种更紧迫的问题浮现出来。这关乎神经多样性运动的整套策略都预设了一个仍能听见权利诉求的世界。更具体而言，它预设了一个自由主义的、基于规则的秩序——在这个秩序中，权利话语仍具合法性：机构无论多么不完善，仍可被诉诸；而承认无论多么不均衡，仍能转化为实质性的让步。

This order, however, is not timeless. It was the product of a specific historical settlement, forged in the aftermath of the Second World War. The horrors of fascism and mass war, plus the existence of the Soviet Union as a possible alternative for workers to turn towards, produced a consensus that liberal democracy required both economic stabilisation and institutional constraint. Hence the Keynesian compromise; the emergence of bodies like the United Nations; and the articulation of documents such as the Universal Declaration of Human Rights.  
然而，这一秩序并非永恒。它是二战后特定历史契约的产物。法西斯主义与大规模战争的恐怖，加上苏联作为工人可能转向的替代选择的存在，催生了一种共识：自由民主既需要经济稳定，也需要制度约束。于是有了凯恩斯主义的妥协；联合国等机构的出现；以及《世界人权宣言》等文件的制定。

For our purposes here, what is important is that it was within this framework that twentieth-century civil rights movements became thinkable, and, eventually, through great collective struggle and sacrifice, successful. By contrast, the neurodiversity movement arrived later, at the moment when that settlement was already unravelling. By the 1990s, Keynesianism has given way to neoliberalism. Welfare states were hollowed out. Housing, healthcare, and education had become sites of increasing precarity. For many, the experience of what would later be named neurodivergence was inseparable from this broader degradation of social conditions.  
就我们此处的讨论而言，关键在于，正是在这一框架下，二十世纪的民权运动才变得可以想象，并最终通过伟大的集体斗争与牺牲取得了成功。相比之下，神经多样性运动出现得更晚，恰逢这一社会契约已然瓦解之际。到 20 世纪 90 年代，凯恩斯主义已让位于新自由主义。福利国家被掏空。住房、医疗和教育日益成为不稳定的领域。对许多人来说，后来被称为神经殊异性的体验，与这种社会条件的整体恶化密不可分。

In this context, claiming disability status, demanding rights on that basis, became a rational strategy, as I detailed above. But even as this strategy scaled up during the 2010s, the ground beneath was already shifting. While the 2008 financial crisis did not immediately end neoliberalism, it did fatally undermine its legitimacy. And what followed was not a return to the post-war settlement, but a vacuum that allowed new political forms to emerge, with a resurgence of both left and right political movements alongside a collapse of the centre ground.  
在此背景下，主张残障身份并以此为基础要求权利成为了一种理性策略，正如我上文所述。但即便这一策略在 2010 年代不断扩展，其根基已然动摇。2008 年金融危机虽未立即终结新自由主义，却致命地削弱了其合法性。随之而来的并非战后秩序的回归，而是一个真空地带——新的政治形态得以涌现，左右翼政治运动同时复兴，中间立场随之瓦解。

Unfortunately, the form of politics that is far better funded and has been winning has come from the radical right. It has been far less invested in the language of rights, except in cases of co-optation such as ‘men’s rights’ activism or the ways women’s rights discourses have been used to exclude trans women or used by [femonationalists](https://www.transform.review/female-faces-of-fascism/) for Islamophobic purposes. Increasingly, in any case, it has come to discard rights altogether, often with gleeful cruelty. Promises to rip up human rights legislation by far-right politicians are met with increasingly bold support. The genocide in Palestine has been livestreamed for years, to mass protest around the world, while its perpetrators still retain full support from increasingly illiberal Western governments. The world we are entering is not yet fully determined, but it is taking an increasingly clear form. In this emerging post-liberal landscape, the very premises of liberal neurodiversity politics, this being the post-war liberal order, has faltered. Those still benefiting from its protections, or advocating as if it remained intact, are living on borrowed time.  
遗憾的是，资金更为充裕且持续占据上风的政体形式来自激进右翼。除了"男性权利"运动这类被收编的案例，或女性权利话语被用来排斥跨性别女性、被女性民族主义者用于仇视伊斯兰教目的的情况外，右翼对权利话语的投入微乎其微。无论如何，它正日益以残忍的欢愉姿态彻底抛弃权利概念。极右翼政客撕毁人权立法的承诺正获得愈发大胆的支持。巴勒斯坦的种族灭绝被直播数年，引发全球大规模抗议，而施暴者仍持续获得日益非自由化的西方政府的全力支持。我们正在进入的世界尚未完全定型，但其轮廓正日益清晰。在这个新兴的后自由主义格局中，自由派神经多样性政治的根本前提——即战后自由主义秩序——已然动摇。那些仍受其保护、或假装其完好无损而继续倡导的人，不过是在苟延残喘。

Couple with this the fact that the ruling class has been looking for ways to roll back neurodivergent rights for the simple reason that they see accommodations as costly and as negatively impacting productivity. So far, [as I wrote about previously](https://substack.com/home/post/p-180869012), a big part of the ruling class backlash has focused on “overdiagnosis” discourses to try and delegitimise claims to being disabled. Similarly, attacks on the very concept of “neurodivergence” as well as specific diagnoses are becoming more common. Reactionaries like psychiatrist Sami Timimi, who have been doing that for a long time unnoticed, have gotten major book deals and so on in recent years. Their tactic is the same as that taken by ‘gender critical’ feminists in relation to trans people: undermine the legitimacy of the classification to effect legal undermining of rights. In short, the shift towards the far right in politics internationally is the context in which these arguments are gaining traction and becoming mainstreamed. And the main outcome of this will be the diminishment or loss of mitigating rights for millions of people.  
与此同时，统治阶级一直在寻找削弱神经殊异者权利的方法，原因很简单——他们视合理便利为成本高昂且会拖累生产力。正如我此前所写，统治阶级的反扑很大程度上聚焦于"过度诊断"话语，试图否定残障主张的合法性。同样，对"神经殊异性"这一概念本身以及具体诊断的攻击也日益常见。像精神病学家萨米·蒂米米这类长期暗中从事此类活动的反动分子，近年来已获得重要图书出版合约等资源。他们的策略与"性别批判"女权主义者针对跨性别者的手段如出一辙：通过破坏分类的合法性，从法律层面削弱权利。简言之，国际政治向极右翼的转向，正是这些论点获得关注并成为主流的背景。而其主要后果，将是数百万人的权利保障被削弱或丧失。

## The Urgent Need for An Alternative亟需另寻出路

The thing is with far-right mass politics, fascist or otherwise, a return to the centre is not an alternative. It was decades of centrism and austerity, with no real left mass politics, that led to the situation where so many people [lurched right in the 2010s](https://americanaffairsjournal.org/2017/11/progressive-neoliberalism-trump-beyond/). Those who advocate [more of the same](https://www.bostonreview.net/forum/the-future-of-neurodiversity/make-neurodiversity-boring/) technocratic centrism when it comes to disability seem to have missed this.  
问题在于，极右翼大众政治（无论是法西斯主义还是其他形式）的兴起，使得回归中间路线并非可行之选。正是数十年的中间主义与紧缩政策，加之缺乏真正的左翼大众政治，才导致 2010 年代如此多的人转向右翼。那些在残障议题上仍鼓吹技术官僚式中间路线的人，似乎完全忽略了这一点。

To be sure, centrists may still be able to win some elections, bringing together coalitions that mainly vote for them on the basis that they are not quite as bad as the far right. But at best that just leads to increased disaffection, since nothing changes for the better even when they win. As we saw in the U.S., Biden won but then oversaw a genocide, an [increase](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/c36e41dx425o) in migrant deportations, and a continuation of managed decline, after which Trump was predictably elected back in, going in a much more extreme direction than his first term. In any case, as a century of analysis from Marxists and the [Black radical tradition](https://www.bostonreview.net/articles/long-shadow-racial-fascism/) show, albeit in different ways, fascism is the underbelly of liberal capitalism that emerges in moments of crisis, not its opposite. And the direction things are going, it seems likely that without a mass politics in favour of a genuine alternative, things may get far worse than Trump.  
诚然，中间派或许仍能赢得部分选举，通过组建联盟让选民主要基于"他们总比极右翼强"的理由投票支持。但充其量这只会加剧民众的不满，因为即便他们获胜，情况也丝毫不会好转。正如我们在美国所见，拜登胜选后却纵容种族灭绝、增加移民驱逐、延续有管理的衰退，随后特朗普不出所料地重新当选，其执政方向比第一任期更为极端。无论如何，正如马克思主义者和黑人激进传统长达一个世纪的分析（尽管方式不同）所揭示的：法西斯主义是自由资本主义在危机时刻暴露的阴暗面，而非其对立面。而按照当前的发展趋势，若缺乏支持真正替代方案的大众政治，情况可能比特朗普时代还要糟糕得多。

As before, my argument is that for neurodivergent people to have any hope, we need a mass shift from liberal neurodiversity politics to a radical left neurodiversity politics. That is, we need a movement for collective power and socialism, and which builds a recognition that neurodivergent people require socialism into the basis of its theory and organising. But now it’s not just that we need to do this because we will never be able to attain more than mere mitigations for neurodivergents under capitalism. It is because the strategies and forms of politics we have relied on for even rights-based mitigations assumed a world that no longer exists.  
如前所述，我的论点是：若神经殊异群体尚存希望，就必须实现从自由主义神经多样性政治向激进左翼神经多样性政治的大规模转向。这意味着我们需要一场追求集体权力与社会主义的运动，并将"神经殊异群体需要社会主义"这一认知融入其理论基础与组织实践。但如今，我们不仅需要这样做——因为在资本主义制度下，神经殊异群体最多只能获得些许缓解措施——更因为即便是争取权利型缓解措施所依赖的政治策略与形式，其预设的世界图景已然不复存在。
